United States Census: Spoken Languages: An overview of United States Multilingualism

The united states is comprised of a vibrant melting pot of foreign cultures. On average, there are 350 spoken languages across U.S. Homes. Since 1980, language specific data has been collected to monitor many facets of lingualism in the U.S. The below information was taken directly from the United States Census through the American Community Survey program used to collect language data.

Multilingualism by State Languages spoken at home other than English.

Based on census data collected between 2009 - 2013. The largest linguist melting pot in the country is california surpassing the next largest linguist state (texas) by an additional 80 percent. Multilingualism is seen less across the southern united states and rarely across the midwest states.


Spanish Language Prevalence: US Census records 1980 - 2010

Data from the U.S. Census from 1980 - 2010 has been collected to show that in the United States there is an average of 350 languages spoken in U.S. homes. Of all languages spoken, Spanish is the second most popularly used and continues to show growth.

Spoken Language Trends Languages spoken at home - age 5 and older 1980 - 2010.

Education Attainment: English Only Speakers U.S. Census 2000.

The data can further be organized to show that in the year 2000 there were 151,154,979 english only speaking participants who had listed educational background. Leading in this area were previous students that had graduated from High School.
